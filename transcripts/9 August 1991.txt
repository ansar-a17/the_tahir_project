From this point of view, having received light from this couplet of the Promised Messiah ﷺ, that the root of every virtue is righteousness, if it remains the root, everything remains. When I thought about it, I found out that the root of Taqwa is in intentions. And on this point, my mind went towards the Hadith of the Promised Messiah ﷺ in which the beginning is as follows, إِنَّمَا الْعَامَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ All deeds are based on intentions. So in fact, these are only two of the same subject. The style of expression is different. The expression has been expressed in different words. But there is only one subject that is being stated. The root of every action of every person is in his intentions. So if that root is Taqwa, then all the buildings of his deeds, whether they reach Surah Surah, they will be acceptable and beautiful in the presence of God. A beautiful and beautiful and solid building will be built. And if the root of Taqwa, if the root of intentions is spoiled, then nothing else remains. If you leave the concept of building and bring the example of trees from the concept of roots in front of your eyes, then the root that is sick, its stem is also sick, its leaves are also sick, its fruits are also sick. And the tree with sick roots, now whatever you want to do, its treatment is not possible. Except that it should be thrown away from the roots. Or such a medicine should be given, which gets into the roots, and cures some of the root diseases. I have had such an experience in Barha Azim Indara. There are many diseases of plants and trees, which attack the leaves, and fruits, and branches. Their treatment is possible. But such a tree, which starts drying up from the stems, and its disease acts downwards. That is, starting from the edges, downwards. Such trees are always infected with root diseases. And until the root is not concerned, no treatment is possible for that tree.
